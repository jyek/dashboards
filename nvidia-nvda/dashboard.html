NVIDIA (NVDA)

Global leader in GPU-accelerated computing. Dominant AI infrastructure supplier.

Business Model

  • Data Center (~85%+ of revenue) — AI training and inference GPUs (H100, B100, Blackwell), networking (Mellanox/InfiniBand), and DGX systems
  • Gaming (~10%) — GeForce desktop and notebook GPUs
  • Automotive (~1-2%) — DRIVE platform for autonomous driving and infotainment
  • Professional Visualization (~2-3%) — Quadro/RTX GPUs for design and engineering

Key Drivers

  • Data Center compute — GPU unit volumes and blended ASP (H100 → Blackwell transition)
  • Cloud CapEx cycle — hyperscaler capital spending directly drives DC GPU demand
  • Gross margin — product mix shift toward higher-margin DC GPUs
  • Networking — Mellanox/InfiniBand growing with scale-out AI clusters

Model Flow

Datacenter (cloud capex, GPU units, ASPs by architecture) → Worksheet (segment revenues: DC, Gaming, Auto, ProViz → consolidated P&L, margins, EPS) → NVDA (detailed financials: P&L → BS → CF, unit economics) → Gaming (desktop/notebook GPU units, ASPs, crypto) → Automotive (DRIVE + infotainment revenue buildup)

Currency

USD (millions). Fiscal year ends January (e.g. FY2026 = Feb 2025 – Jan 2026).
